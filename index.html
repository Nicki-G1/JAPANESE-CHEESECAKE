JAPANESE CHEESECAKE / スフレチーズケーキ
INGREDIENTS INSTRUCTIONS CONTACT

As fluffy as a French soufflé and as moist as an American cheesecake, this Japanese cheesecake definitely belongs in every cheesecake lover's recipe repertoire! Note: For the tips & tricks on baking Japanese Cheesecake, it's best to read the detailed blogpost first. Country & Region: Japan, Japanese Pieces

INGREDIENTS / 材料:

Ingredients for a 20cm / 8 inch diameter Baking Pan springform
  • 5 medium size eggs
  • 100 g finest baking sugar / powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoon vanilla extract or 2 sachet vanilla sugar max. 16g
  • 50 g flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon Salt
  • 15 g cornflour
  • 150 g cream cheese double cream
  • 60 ml / 2,1unze full cream milk
  • 40 g butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
Optional
  • some icing sugar to sprinkle
  • Rhubarb and wild berry compote or fresh strawberries

INSTRUCTIONS / 手順

Preparation makes work easier later

Preheat the oven to 125°C / 257°F top/bottom heat. Line the bottom of a round cake tin with baking paper and grease the sides. This cheesecake bakes very high, so you should use an appropriately high tin. Depending on the cake tin you use, you can also line the walls with baking paper and cover the tin with two layers of aluminium foil if it is not closed.

Preparing the cheesecake dough

Separate the eggs. Whisk the egg yolks with half the sugar, lemon zest, lemon juice and vanilla extract and set aside. Whisk the egg whites later with the second half of the sugar. Mix the flour, salt and starch in a separate bowl and sieve the mixture several times.

Put the cream cheese in a small saucepan with the milk and butter and let it melt over a gentle heat. When the cream cheese and butter have completely dissolved in the milk, remove the pan from the heat and slowly and evenly stir in the flour mixture with a whisk. Then stir in the egg yolk mixture until you have a smooth, homogeneous batter.

Whip the egg whites in a clean, grease-free bowl for about 30 seconds. Add the cream of tartar or a squeeze of lemon juice. Continue beating the egg whites, slowly adding the remaining sugar. Beat the egg whites until soft peaks form (about 3 to 4 minutes). Fold the beaten egg whites into the remaining ingredients in two to three stages. Mix until an even, smooth dough has formed, without stirring out too much air.

Cheesecake from the bain marie

Pour the batter into your prepared baking tin. Let the tin gently touch your work surface two or three times.Place the filled cake tin in a baking dish or a sufficiently large, ovenproof pot. Pour 2-3 cm / 1,2 inch of hot water into it. Place the water bath with the cake tin in it on a baking tray and place it slightly lower than the centre in the preheated oven.

The 3 baking phases of the cheesecake

The first baking phase begins at 125°C/ 257°F top/bottom heat, to which you have already preheated your oven. Allow the cheesecake to heat up and rise a little. IMPORTANT: Only open the oven door as described during the baking process. Otherwise the soufflé-like cheesecake could collapse. Increase the oven temperature to 150°C after 20 minutes. Keep the oven door closed.

After 15 minutes, open the oven door briefly for about 10 seconds and reduce the temperature to 100°C / 212 °F for the third baking phase. Now bake the cheesecake for another 40 minutes.

Then switch off the oven. Open the oven door once briefly and then close it again. Leave the cheesecake to cool in the closed oven for 10 minutes.Carefully take the cake out of the oven. It will probably have come away from the edge by itself. If not, just give it a little help. Carefully turn down the cake tin and let the cheesecake slide onto your hand or a cake rack (be careful, it's hot!). Then turn it right side up again.

Time to eat and enjoy

Unlike other cheesecake and cheesecake variations, you can actually serve the Japanese cheesecake warm. It goes well with either a fruity compote or fresh fruit. But a little icing sugar is also a perfect finishing touch. If you don't want to eat the cheesecake right away, you can store it in the fridge for 3 days after cooling.
